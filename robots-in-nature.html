Robots in Nature

Human-Robot-Environment Interaction for Advanced Ecosystem Services

...

Aim

To explore the design of robotic systems that can deliver data-driven, precision ecosystem services to help address serious environmental issues & enrich everyday life

Objective

  • Explore interactions between humans, robots, & the environment
  • Develop robotic systems that respond to real-time data from the environment & precisely implement optimized solutions
  • Generate enhanced ecosystem services

Project Program Fit

NSF NRI-2.0: Ubiquitous Collaborative Robots

"It is not clear however, if the proposed projects align with the priorities of the funders/sponsors."

  • Scalability: scalability of operating a robotic greenwall or urban robotic farming system within the built environment and near humans
  • Societal impact: the impact of robotic automation of ecosystem services on humans’ relationship with and perception of robots
  • Lowering barriers to entry: through open source software and hardware
  • Customizability

    • Project Program Fit

    EPA P3: People, Prosperity, and Planet

  • Student research on real world challenges: robotic planting may improve crop yields and may also support efforts to maintain and improve biodiversity, protecting against catastrophic system failures
  • Improve quality of life, economy, or environment: the smart greenwall is aimed directly at improving quality of life in urban settings

    • Project Program Fit

    SERDP/ESTCP Munitions Response

  • Demonstrate technologies that can characterize, remediate, and scientifically manage sites affected by military munitions: robotic planting supports restoration of damaged plant ecosystems on inaccessible sites
  • Sites range from acres to tens of thousands of acres: principles of robotic locomotion, robotic terrain manipulation, and safe interaction with plants will enable the development of systems that permit the efficient remediation of these inaccessible sites

    • Project Program Fit

    NAS Gulf Research Program – Thriving Communities

  • Understanding communities and their systems: aerial robotic surveying for mapping coastal land loss and restoration
  • Actionable information: robotic planting enables access to new sites and should enable more cost-effective environmental restoration at large scales
